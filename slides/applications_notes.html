Heat exchanger

In the previous example, we demonstrated that work can be extracted from the natural cooling of a hot cup of coffee. If we just let the coffee cool by itself, then we will have lost all that potential work. Exergy is lost every time a transformation is allowed to naturally occur without trying to extract work from the process. One example of this is the transfer of heat between two fluid streams in a counter-current heat exchanger.

A hot liquid stream with a mass flowrate \dot{m}H and a heat capacity Cp,H enters the heat exchanger at a temperature TH1. This is used to heat a cold stream with a mass flowrate \dot{m}C and heat capacity Cp,C from an initial temperature TC1 to a target temperature of TC2.

From an energy balance over the heat exchanger, we can determine the exit temperature of the hot stream.

Limitations of Exergy analysis

  • Only tells us what can be achieved, not how to achieve it.
  • Technology may not exist to perform required transformations
  • Neglects time
  • Assumes that the ``environment'' (reference state) is constant.
  • Neglects economic or social factors
